## Overview

This plan is organized around two sequential priorities:

**Priority 1 — Stasis Retention (Day 1):** Stasis is the highest-growth brand with the strongest moat and the clearest lifecycle gap. It needs its own purpose-built lifecycle engine — not borrowed Thesis patterns. That work starts immediately and should be generating measurable results by Day 60.

**Priority 2 — Thesis Retention (Day 30–60):** Thesis retention has dropped significantly and needs a triage pass. Quick wins where the cost/benefit is clear get implemented. Everything else gets scoped into a funded roadmap with a clear sequence.

By Day 90, both brands have functioning lifecycle engines, the team and resource structure reflects what an AI-first function actually needs, and retention is reporting a measurable number — not just a narrative.

### Stasis Retention: First Builds

The post-purchase design A/B test (5.5% CR vs. 1.7%) is running and winning. That is a design win on top of an existing structure, not a full lifecycle. The full Stasis lifecycle journey — why cortisol matters, what Stasis is doing in the body, when to expect results, what it means if you don't feel it yet — does not exist.

**Builds by Day 60:**
- **Stasis lifecycle content layer**: A post-purchase series that educates on the mechanism, sets realistic timelines, surfaces social proof, and drives habit formation. This is not a redesign of what's already testing — it is the strategic content that should sit around it. Education is what keeps Stasis subscribers through month 2 and 3.
- **Stasis at-risk re-engagement**: No flow currently exists for this. Build with a properly defined engagement-signal segment and a holdout.
- **Stasis lifecycle SMS**: Not in any current project plan. A post-purchase SMS complement to the email series is the minimum. Suppression and cadence rules before anything goes live.

**Every new flow goes through `playbooks/lifecycle-audit.md` before launch.**

**Stasis segments to formalize:**
- Stasis active subscribers (full subscriber base, not just L30 purchasers)
- Stasis at-risk (engagement-signal based)
- Stasis winback eligible (per `playbooks/winback.md`)

**Immediate audit — what can be fixed in a week that materially moves the number:**

## What This Plan Is Not

- **This is not an app development plan.** Dan is managing the app project with an intern. Lifecycle will contribute feedback when asked.
- **This is not a content or organic social plan.** The content machine and podcast are Dan-directed. Lifecycle consumes that output — it does not produce it.
- **This is not a retail or Amazon plan.** Mass retail is ~April 2027. Amazon is growing but channel-managed. Channel-aware cohorts are a Q3/Q4 lifecycle conversation.
- **This is not fixed.** If the first 30 days reveal something that changes the sequence, the plan gets updated.
